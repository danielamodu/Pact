PRIVACY
PROTOCOL

At Pact, privacy is not a featureΓÇöit is our baseline. As a decentralized autonomous payment layer, we are committed to processing your data with the same cryptographic integrity that secures your transactions. This policy outlines how we handle the minimal data necessary to provide our cross-chain subscription services.

[ 01.1 What We Collect ]

We primarily collect public blockchain identifiers, including wallet addresses and transaction hashes. We do not collect names, physical addresses, or government-issued IDs.

[ 01.2 How We Collect ]

Information is collected automatically through blockchain RPC providers when you interact with our smart contracts or link your wallet to our dashboard.

01.3 Cookie Usage Policy

We use essential session tokens stored locally to maintain your wallet connection. No third-party tracking cookies or marketing pixels are used on the Pact interface.

How We Use Your Data

Data is utilized exclusively to facilitate recurring billing triggers, cross-chain balance verification, and transaction status reporting. We do not sell user data to data brokers or advertising networks.

Security Measures

Off-chain metadata is encrypted using AES-256 standards. Our relayer nodes use hardware security modules (HSM) to handle execution triggers without exposing underlying private keys.

Access & Deletion

You have the right to request a summary of the metadata we hold. While blockchain data is immutable, you can request the removal of any off-chain linkage from our centralized relay databases.

Data Portability

You can export your subscription history and permission logs in JSON format at any time through the Subscriber Dashboard.

To maintain high availability, Pact integrates with the following types of infrastructure providers:

Blockchain NodesAlchemy, Infura, Base RPC
Cloud ComputingAWS (Encrypted Relayers)
AnalyticsSelf-Hosted Plausible (Privacy First)
